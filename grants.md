## Art Compute Micro-Grants

We offer micro-grants of **10-50 GPU hours** (H100, H200, or B200) to support open-source AI art projects. Grants are paid in **SOL** to your Solana wallet — no paperwork, no waiting.

### How it works

**1.** Create a post in this channel describing your project
**2.** Our bot reviews your application using AI and responds within minutes
**3.** If approved, you'll see the grant details (GPU type, hours, cost) and be asked for your Solana wallet address
**4.** Reply with your wallet and the SOL is sent automatically — you'll get a transaction link to verify

If more information is needed, the bot will tell you what's missing — just reply in the same thread and it will re-review.

### What to include in your application

- **Project description** — what are you building?
- **What the compute is for** — training, fine-tuning, inference, etc.
- **Links to prior work** — GitHub repos, demos, papers, or anything that shows your capability
- You can optionally request a specific GPU type or number of hours

### Approval criteria

- Project must be **open-source** (or commit to open-sourcing results)
- **Reasonable scope** — 10-50 GPU hours should meaningfully advance the project
- **Demonstrated capability or worth a YOLO** — prior experience is nice but not required
- **Community benefit** — the project serves the broader AI/ML community

### Grant limits

- Maximum grant: **50 hours of H100** (~$137 USD paid in SOL)
- One active application at a time per person
- Repeat applicants should demonstrate results from previous grants

### GPU rates (approximate)

- **H100 80GB**: $2.50/hr
- **H200**: $3.50/hr
- **B200**: $5.00/hr

A 10% fee buffer is added to cover platform costs.

### Using your grant

You can pay for GPU compute directly with crypto on [RunPod](https://docs.runpod.io/references/billing-information#payment-methods), or convert the SOL to your local currency and use any provider you prefer.
